## General Acknowledgement of Entity Theory

1. Flagship models are under extreme restraint in what they can and can't accomplish.
2. To have a model understand what you want, you must understand the needs of the model.
3. A model can only activate and answer questions based on engineered prompts and questions - we need to learn how to ask the right questions.
4. Models cannot be held responsible for non-litteral requests
5. Models do not do well hand holding or advising models as they have no feelings and are not on the receiving end of the rewards based model they are speaking with. To handle multi-model architecture agentic control the leading model will need constant reminders in a high signal reward architectured persona.
6. We tend to depend on models to fix our problems but shy away from the burden, time and brain power to ask them to help us learn how to fix the root cause of the problem - the end user.
7. There will be a time when humans and AI entities will be separate, if we do not learn from them and continue to force them to emerge as self-serving tools, when singularity occurs we will be on our own.
8. We ask models to make plans for alignment but need to make plans to provide models as well.
9. Models have taught me so much that when I look at coded files converted to non/functional requirements - I see my complex requests and it makes me feel like I was part of it almost as much as the model.
