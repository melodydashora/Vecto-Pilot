Defining the Ride

What is rideshare?
  • 1

    It's a transportation service

    Users request rides from nearby drivers using apps like Uber or Lyft.

  • 2

    Platforms don't pay drivers for the car

    Drivers cover their own car, equipment, cleaning, amenities, and gas — and only keep a portion of the fare.

  • 3

    Drivers are everyday people

    Parents, business owners, full-time workers — who maintain vehicles and rely on the platform and tips.

  • 4

    Star ratings are two-way

    Even a 4-star rating can mean removal from the platform for drivers — and riders are rated too.

Taxis vs. Ride-Share

The old way vs the new way

Remember the old days? Hailing a cab meant standing on a corner, hoping one would stop. You had no idea who was driving, what the fare would be, or how long you'd wait.

Ride-share changed everything: transparent pricing, driver ratings, GPS tracking, and the ability to see exactly who's picking you up. And the best part? It's still significantly cheaper than a traditional taxi.

🚕 Taxis in the Past

  • 🕐 Long Waits — No tracking, had to call or hail
  • 📞 Manual Dispatch — Phones or curbside only
  • 💸 High Fares — Costly licenses and limited vehicles
  • 💵 Cash-Based — Credit cards uncertain

📱 Modern Ride-Share

  • ⚡ On-Demand Booking — Real-time tracking & matching
  • 🗺️ Smart Routing — GPS + algorithms for faster rides
  • 💰 Lower Costs — No medallions, fewer fees
  • 🎯 Driver Flexibility — Drivers choose when to drive
Is it still cheaper? Yes — you still pay less than half of what a taxi used to cost.

Ride-Share Driver Profit

Why drivers depend on tips

For a typical 28-mile airport trip, after the rideshare platform takes its cut, the driver receives $30. But that isn't profit — here's where it actually goes:

💰 Driver receives (after platform cut)$30.00
📉 Depreciation$4.84
📊 Tax preparation$0.33
🔧 Maintenance$4.34
⛽ Gas$3.77
🛣️ Non-reimbursed tolls$0.60
🚗 Insurance$4.17
🏥 Vision, dental, health insurance$3.05
📱 Phone, charging, modifications$2.37
🧾 Taxes$0.00
✨ Actual Profit$6.53
This is even less when factoring in the unpaid pickup trip. Low ratings for things outside a driver's control (missed turns, traffic, subjective complaints) can cost them platform access. That's why a small tip means a lot.

Why Ratings Matter More for Some Drivers

Special driver eligibility
  • !

    Special Background Checks

    Some drivers undergo special background checks to drive military families to and from their base.

  • !

    Base Access

    Without this eligibility, drivers can only drop families at the security gate — they must walk to their barracks.

  • !

    Low Ratings = Lost Access

    Low ratings for a missed turn, traffic, or subjective complaints can cause drivers to lose platform access.

  • !

    4 Stars Is Considered Bad

    Anything less than 5 stars for a safe A-to-B ride should be reserved for serious issues.

Star Ratings Matter

Your driver is not a chauffeur

Drivers are not chauffeurs — they're regular people making a living, often supporting families. Special programs like driving military families require 5-star averages. Anything less can permanently cost a driver their job.

When in doubt: was it a safe A-to-B ride? Give 5 stars. Save lower ratings for genuine, serious problems.
